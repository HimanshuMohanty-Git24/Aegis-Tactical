# Aegis-Tactical: Ground Truth Document — Cyber Defense Whitepaper

## Executive Summary

This document outlines the core principles and frameworks used by the Aegis-Tactical
system for identifying and classifying information threats within a defined perimeter.

## Threat Classification Framework

### Tier 1 — Critical Threats
- **State-sponsored disinformation campaigns**: Coordinated efforts by nation-state actors
  to manipulate public perception through fabricated news, deepfakes, and social engineering.
- **Supply chain compromise indicators**: Signals that a trusted software or hardware vendor
  has been infiltrated, potentially affecting downstream consumers.

### Tier 2 — High-Priority Threats
- **AI-generated misinformation**: Content created by generative AI systems designed to
  mislead, including synthetic media, fabricated research papers, and false testimonials.
- **Credential harvesting campaigns**: Phishing operations targeting employees through
  impersonation of legitimate services and trusted contacts.

### Tier 3 — Monitoring-Level Threats
- **Reputation manipulation**: Review bombing, astroturfing, and coordinated inauthentic
  behavior on social media platforms.
- **Data exfiltration signals**: Unusual patterns of data transfer that may indicate
  unauthorized access to sensitive information stores.

## Verification Methodology

The Aegis-Tactical system employs a three-stage verification pipeline:

1. **Source Triangulation**: Every claim is cross-referenced against at least three
   independent sources. Single-source intelligence is flagged as "unverified."

2. **Temporal Consistency**: Information is checked against a timeline of known events.
   Claims that conflict with established timelines receive elevated scrutiny.

3. **Semantic Drift Detection**: The system monitors for subtle changes in narrative
   framing that may indicate coordinated manipulation campaigns. Baseline semantic
   fingerprints are maintained for key topics.

## Rules of Engagement

- The system SHALL NOT take autonomous action that could cause real-world harm.
- All destructive operations (e.g., blocking IPs, revoking credentials) REQUIRE
  human-in-the-loop approval.
- Personally Identifiable Information (PII) discovered during analysis MUST be
  redacted before storage or reporting.
- Confidence scores below 0.7 MUST be flagged for human review.
